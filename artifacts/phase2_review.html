Demand-Forecasting Agent · Phase 2 review

Baseline & the bar

The honest L2 floor for each dataset — and the trivial forecast that replaces it as the target where the floor turns out not to be worth beating. Under volume-weighted WMAPE a zero forecast scores exactly 1.000, so the bar Phase 3 must clear is min(baseline, 1.0). On two of three datasets, the fitted baseline loses to predicting nothing.

The bar for Phase 3

One target per dataset. Where the baseline scores worse than the zero forecast, the bar is 1.000 — beating the baseline there would prove nothing a zero-cost constant hasn't already done.

What the run says

Baseline against both comparators

1 · The headline — every WMAPE against the 1.000 rule

Baseline (L2 + sparse mean-fallback, all series) against the two comparators. The dashed rule is the zero forecast — it is not measured but derived: sum|y−0| / sum(y) ≡ 1. Bars to the right of it are worse than forecasting nothing. Naive and L2 are scored on the modelable (non-B2) series only, so they answer a narrower question than the baseline bar.

2 · By Syntetos–Boylan class — where the aggregate hides

The honesty check on a volume-weighted headline. Every cell at or above the rule is a class the baseline loses to zero on — including A's own intermittent class, which is why A's aggregate pass is carried by its smooth and erratic series rather than earned across the board.

3 · Per-fold stability

Pooled WMAPE at each rolling origin. B and C sit above the rule in all five folds — losing to zero is the stable state, not a one-window artifact.

4 · Dataset A by volume tercile

A's pass is a high-volume phenomenon: its low-volume third is itself above the rule, more than 2× the high third.

Why the target transform wins — and what it is really doing
