Demand-Forecasting Agent · Phase 1 review

EDA signals & dataset selection

Three branch signals computed over every store-item series, and the four sub-datasets chosen from the pattern extremes. Every chart below feeds one downstream decision — a branch threshold or a dataset pick. Thresholds are fit to the dataset's own distribution (format-general, threshold-adaptive); validated on M5, the reference dataset.

What the signals say

Signal distributions

Calibration views (1–3) are drawn over the non-held-out pool — the thresholds we read off never see D. The intermittency map colours every point by its Syntetos–Boylan class.

1 · Zero-share distribution

How zero-heavy each series is, over its active window. Feeds the B1 (Tweedie) cut: at or above the dashed line, zeros dominate enough that a Tweedie objective is warranted over squared-error regression.

2 · Intermittency map — ADI vs CV²

Average demand interval (how often it sells) against squared variability of non-zero sizes. Feeds the B1-vs-B2 split: the vertical sparse cut separates modelable intermittent series (Tweedie) from too-sparse ones that fall back to a baseline. Quadrant lines are the standard SB cutoffs (1.32, 0.49).

3 · Weekly seasonality — day-of-week variance explained (η²)

Fraction of each series' demand variance explained by which day of the week it is (deterministic, spike-honest — replaces the config-fragile STL strength). Feeds the B3 cut — and the spread is stark: the median item's day-of-week explains ~1% of its variance, so the seasonal-feature branch fires for only a small minority. For most series, calendar features carry seasonality instead.

4 · Spike ratio

Fraction of active days that are demand spikes (MAD-based, promo/event driven). Feeds the critic's plausible-magnitude bound — spikes are real demand, and their tail sets what forecast magnitudes count as sane.

Decisions for your review

1 · Confirm the B pick — CA_2 × HOUSEHOLD_2 for Tweedie.
The rule chose the most intermittent-class-dominant modelable group (93% intermittent), which is a purer Tweedie showcase than a HOBBIES_1 cell. Accept, or prefer a HOBBIES cell?
2 · The seasonal branch (B3) barely fires.
Day-of-week explains only ~1% of the median item's demand variance (P90 η² ≈ 0.042), so B3 tags ~10% of series. Options for Phase 3: keep the low-value seasonal toggle, drop it and let calendar features (wday/events/SNAP) carry seasonality, or assess seasonality on aggregated series where the weekly signal is stronger.
3 · Threshold values (all data-relative to this dataset).
Sparse cut ADI ≥ (P90), Tweedie cut zero-share ≥ (P60 of non-sparse — genuinely high), seasonal cut η² ≥ (P90). Fit on the non-D pool; a different dataset re-fits them. Syntetos–Boylan cutoffs stay absolute. Adjust any before Phase 2?
